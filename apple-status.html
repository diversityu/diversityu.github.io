Apple's Current Status

The next present issue exists within the greater scope of Apple employment. This issue encompasses all employees, including retail, management, and everything in between. The adjacent graph presents the issue of gender and race in a pie and bar graph respectively. Large discrepancies are obviously present, but it is important to remember that these numbers are from the total employment, whereas the issue is amplified at the senior management level. “Only 32% of the total workforce is female, which is low compared to globally recognized companies such as Walmart (43%) or JP Morgan Chase (55%).” This quantifies Apple against two other globally recognized, massive companies. These companies would be considered within different business divisions, i.e. general retail and banking respectively, however we believe that it is important to compare Apple to these companies because they are successful global leaders in their fields and Apple should strive to keep up with the standard of other companies in order to remain strong in terms of market share, and profitable for their stakeholders.

In addition to the above facts, we can also divide Apple into different divisions and delve into the issue further. The following graph divides apple into leadership, non-tech, and tech categories to further promote the issue of gender equity within the workforce. There is a clear discrepancy between the two genders, especially in the leadership and tech divisions.
